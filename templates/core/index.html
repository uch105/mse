What are the key differences between austenitic and martensitic stainless steels?
Austenitic stainless steels have a face-centered cubic structure, are non-magnetic, and contain high levels of chromium and nickel. They offer excellent corrosion resistance. Martensitic stainless steels have a body-centered tetragonal structure, are magnetic, and contain higher carbon levels. They can be heat treated for high strength but have lower corrosion resistance.
Calculate the theoretical density of copper given its atomic properties.
Copper has an FCC crystal structure with atomic radius 0.128 nm. The unit cell edge length a = 4r/√2 = 0.361 nm. With 4 atoms per unit cell and atomic mass 63.55 g/mol, theoretical density = (4 × 63.55) / (6.022×10²³ × (3.61×10⁻⁸)³) = 8.96 g/cm³.
